By Manojna Maddipatla and John Miller (Reuters) - One of medicine's costliest treatment areas could become even more pricey as Biogen seeks to layer its $750,000 spinal muscular atrophy drug on top of $2.1 million-per-patient Novartis gene therapy Zolgensma.In a call with analysts after Biogen's announcement, Novartis Chief Executive Vas Narasimhan did not directly address whether patients had adequate responses to Zolgensma or if they could benefit from Spinraza treatment after gene therapy.
