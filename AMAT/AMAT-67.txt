Stocks recovered from steep losses early Friday to close higher, despite data showing U.S. April retail sales plunged more than forecasts and news the Trump administration will block shipments of semiconductors to China’s Huawei Technologies, stoking fears of renewed trade tensions.Despite its supply-side forces faring better at present compared to demand-side indicators, it’s evident that China is only managing to tiptoe out of the lockdown gates as opposed to racing out of the blocks, even if the data does point to signs of recovery,” said Han Tan, market analyst at FXTM, in a note.
