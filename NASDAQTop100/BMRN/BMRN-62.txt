BioMarin Pharmaceutical Inc. BMRN announced that it is planning to file regulatory applications in the third quarter of 2020, seeking approval for achondroplasia candidate, vosoritide, in the United States and Europe following its meetings with health authorities.The regulatory applications will be filed based on data from successfully completed phase III study, which evaluated vosoritide as a treatment for achondroplasia, the most common form of disproportionate short stature in humans or dwarfism, in children aged 5 to 14 for 52 weeks.
