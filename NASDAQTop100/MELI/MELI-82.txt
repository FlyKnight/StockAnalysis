By Gabriela Mello SAO PAULO, May 4 (Reuters) - Brazil's battered retailers are starting to reopen after weeks of coronavirus lockdown but may exit the crisis transformed, with the e-commerce sector strengthened and brick-and-mortar chains facing an uphill path to normality."We have been working quite hard to reduce our spending and grant discounts to ease (tenants') cash restraints in April and May," Iguatemi's chief financial officer, Cristina Betts, said in a recent call with analysts.
