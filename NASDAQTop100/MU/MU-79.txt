By Neha Malara and Stephen Nellis (Reuters) - Micron Technology Inc on Monday forecast current-quarter revenue above Wall Street estimates as home-bound employees and students spur demand for its chips that power notebooks and data centers, sending its shares up 6% in after-market trading."Proximity to existing sites just makes it easier to de-bug issues that may come up as we bring up some of these products to high-volume manufacturing," Sumit Sadana, Micron's chief business officer, told Reuters in an interview.
