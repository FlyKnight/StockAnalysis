“With consumers now having had three months to adjust to ‘the new normal,’ we are seeing signs that online purchasing trends formed during the pandemic may see permanent adoption,” said Taylor Schreiner, director at Adobe Digital Insights.“According to the Adobe survey conducted to help inform the Digital Economy Index, one in four consumers say they’ve had a negative experience shopping online, over the past three months, suggesting retailers have an opportunity to improve their site experiences.” Among other insights from the DEI: For its analysis of the DEI, Adobe examines apparel, appliances, books, computers, electronic, flowers and related gifts, furniture and bedding, groceries, home and garden, jewelry, medical equipment and supplies, non-prescription drug, office supplies, personal-care products, pet products, sporting goods, tools, home improvement and toys.
