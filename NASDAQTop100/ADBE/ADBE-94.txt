The S&P 500 tumbled nearly 6% on Thursday and the three main indexes recorded their worst day since mid-March, as the Federal Reserve's indication of a long road to recovery and rising COVID-19 cases in the United States cast a pall over investor bets on a swift economic rebound."The volatility is extremely high and there's an enormous amount of uncertainty," said Randy Frederick, vice president of trading and derivatives for Charles Schwab in Austin, Texas.
