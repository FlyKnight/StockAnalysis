In an interview with Barron’s, Citrix CEO David Henshall noted the company had a strong quarter across the board, with material outperformance on revenues, earnings per share, and cash flow.“Despite an expected tempering from peak demand seen in March and early April, we continue to see rising probability of sustained growth for work-from-home-related technologies ...as customers plan for the next phase of their digital transformation and begin shifting virtual desktops to the cloud.”  Citi analyst Walter Pritchard wrote that “strong topline and subscription fundamentals continue momentum from Q1.” He notes that Citrix provided the biggest increase in full year guidance “in recent memory.” He maintains a Buy rating, and at the time of the note, he wrote expected the stock to trade up.
