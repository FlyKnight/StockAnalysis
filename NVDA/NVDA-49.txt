A year ago, Arm CEO Simon Segars told me that going private under SoftBank had freed the company from public reporting pressures, allowing it to add 2,000 more engineers and to expand its work on IoT and other new initiatives.The reports this past week suggested that SoftBank has hired Goldman Sachs to investigate strategic options for ARM, which could include selling the company outright or taking it back to the public markets several years ahead of schedule.
